Mahere Mahi Toi

You can’t travel far around the Otago Polytechnic without stumbling across one of the many art installations that populate our campus. Until now there has been no resource which allows people to navigate this carefully curated collection of artwork. Mahere Mahi Toi has been developed in collaboration with the Dunedin School of Art to address this opportunity.

As an application Mahere Mahi Toi features an interactive map, curated routes, and insights into the works and artists behind them, providing users with not only a wayfinding tool but also a deeper connection to the creative and cultural elements of our campus.

Mahere Mahi Toi has been designed in collaboration with the Dunedin School of Art to provide an interactive map which allows users to explore the art installations around the Otago Polytechnic campus.

Kia ora,

I'm George, an aspiring UX designer! I have just completed the Otago Polytechnic School of Design Honours Programme which compliments my Bachelor of Communication Design from the same school. I'm fortunate enough to have dabbled in a wide range of fields, including, tertiary lecturing, health sciences, trade, beauty therapy, hospitality, management, and retail. While varied, all of these experiences have strengthened my communication skills and given me a unique lens to wield while problem solving.

I see myself as someone who is driven by eclectic creativity with a strong appreciation for authenticity. I love a challenge and like to approach them with as much relevant research as I can, to help efficiently find solutions.

I'm open to opportunities and flexible about my future so don't hesitate to get in touch if you have a creative goal that we can achieve together.
